CLINICAL INFORMATION: 63-year-old male presenting with seizures and progressive neurological deficits. MRI
demonstrated a ring-enhancing mass lesion within the left frontal lobe.
MACROSCOPIC DESCRIPTION: Received in formalin: Multiple fragments of soft, grey-tan haemorrhagic tissue, aggregate
2.8 × 1.5 × 0.9 cm. Tissue submitted entirely for processing. Blocks: A1–A4.
MICROSCOPIC DESCRIPTION: Sections show a highly cellular infiltrating glial neoplasm demonstrating marked nuclear
pleomorphism, brisk mitotic activity (>10 mitoses/10 HPF), microvascular proliferation (endothelial multi-layering, glomeruloid
vascular tufts), and geographic necrosis with pseudopalisading. Perineuronal satellitosis and subpial spread are identified.
The surrounding neuropil shows reactive astrogliosis.
IMMUNOHISTOCHEMISTRY: GFAP: Strong diffuse positive IDH1 R132H: Negative ATRX: Loss of nuclear expression p53:
Wild-type pattern Ki-67: Approximately 38.1% positivity in hotspot areas EGFR: Overexpressed (3+) PTEN: Loss of
expression
MOLECULAR PROFILE: MGMT promoter methylation (pyrosequencing): Unmethylated IDH1/2 sequencing (next-generation
sequencing panel): Wild-type TERT promoter mutation: C250T detected CDKN2A/B homozygous deletion (FISH): Not
detected EGFR amplification (FISH): Detected, copy number 23 PTEN deletion: Heterozygous deletion detected
DIAGNOSIS: GLIOBLASTOMA MULTIFORME (GBM), WHO CNS Grade IV ICD-O-3: 9440/3 WHO 2021 CNS Tumour
Classification criteria met.
SYNOPSIS: The morphological, immunohistochemical, and molecular findings confirm a WHO Grade IV IDH wild-type
Glioblastoma. The unmethylated MGMT promoter status is an adverse prognostic marker and predicts relative resistance to
temozolomide chemotherapy. Recommend Stupp protocol discussion at neuro-oncology MDT.
